Background

  • FLASH

  • penalized SVD

  • Watanabe et al. (2019) A global overview of pleiotropy and genetic architecture in complex traits

Related work

  • Urbut et al. (2018) MASH

  • Tanigawa et al. (2019) Components of genetic associations across 2,138 phenotypes in the UK Biobank highlight adipocyte biology

Truncated SVD via DeGAs. Separate analysis on different variant sets (1) all vairants, (2) coding vairants (3) protein truncating variants. Retained z-scores for variant-trait pairs with \(p < 0.001\) and standard error below a threshold. I believe other z-scores were set to zero. I don’t think there is any correction for sample overlap.

  • Argelaguet et al. (2018) Multi-Omics Factor Analysis (MOFA)

Intended for integrating different (high dimensioanal) data modalities (e.g. expression, methylation etc), uses individual level data. Loadings can be sparse. Based on group factor analysis (a long list of references! Virtanen et al 2012, Khan et al 2014, Klami et al 2015, Bunte et al 2016, Zhao et al 2016, Leppaaho and Kaski 2017). “(i) fast inference based on a varia- tional approximation, (ii) inference of sparse solutions facilitating interpretation, (iii) efficient handling of missing values and (iv) flex- ible combination of different likelihood models for each data modality, which enables integrating diverse data types such as binary-, count- and continuous-valued data.”

  • Burren et al. (2020) Characterisation of the genetic architecture of immune mediated disease through informed dimension reduction. Uses PCA

  • Grotzinger et al. (2019) Genomic SEM Provides Insights into the Multivariate Genetic Architecture of Complex Traits

  • Runcie and Mukherjee (2013) Dissecting high-dimensional phenotypes with Bayesian sparse factor analysis of genetic covariance matrices

  • Chasman et al. (2020) Pleiotropy based decomposition of genomic risk scores.

  • Gao, Brown, and Engelhardt (2013) A latent factor model with a mixture of sparse and dense factors to model gene expression data with confounding effects

Based on SVD

Data Applications

  • Kettunen et al. (2016) Metabolites

  • Astle et al. (2016) Blood cells data set

  • Khramtsova, Davis, and Stranger (2019) Review article about sex differences in common diseases and common disease genetics.
